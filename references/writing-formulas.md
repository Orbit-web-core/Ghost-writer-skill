# Writing Formulas — Ghost-Writer

Structural patterns for blog writing. Pick one based on your topic.

## PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**Best for:** Pain-point topics

1. **Problem** — State it clearly
2. **Agitate** — Make it hurt. Show the cost.
3. **Solve** — Present the solution. Specific.

**Example:**
- Problem: "Most service businesses have websites that don't generate leads."
- Agitate: "You're paying for a digital brochure while your competitors get the calls you should be getting."
- Solve: "Here's how to build a revenue-generating website that actually converts."

---

## Before-After-Bridge

**Best for:** Vision pieces, category creation

1. **Before** — The world they're in
2. **After** — The world they could be in
3. **Bridge** — How to get there

**Example:**
- Before: "You have a pretty website that nobody finds."
- After: "Your website shows up on Google AND gets recommended by AI."
- Bridge: "Here's the revenue system that makes it happen."

---

## The Ladder

**Best for:** News → meaning

1. **Rung 1:** Specific example
2. **Rung 2:** The principle
3. **Rung 3:** Universal truth

**Example:**
- Rung 1: "Matt's moving company website got 3 calls a month."
- Rung 2: "He had a brochure, not a revenue system."
- Rung 3: "Every service business needs infrastructure, not decoration."

---

## The Inversion

**Best for:** Contrarian takes

1. What everyone believes
2. Evidence for it (be fair)
3. Invert it — what's actually true
4. Prove with stronger evidence

**Example:**
- Belief: "AI will replace web designers."
- Evidence: "Wix and Framer are laying off thousands."
- Inversion: "They're laying off brick-makers, not architects."
- Proof: "The people who know what to build are more valuable than ever."

---

## Formula Selection Guide

| Situation | Formula |
|---|---|
| Breaking news/trend | The Ladder |
| Contrarian take | The Inversion |
| Case study/win | PAS or Before-After-Bridge |
| Vision piece | Before-After-Bridge |
| Response to competitor | The Inversion |
| Personal insight | The Ladder |
