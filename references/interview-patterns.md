# Interview Patterns — Ghost-Writer

Question patterns organized by topic source and situation. Starting points, not scripts. Adapt to the conversation's natural flow.

## Research Integration

Before the interview, keyword research produces: dominant narrative, the gap, key stats, competitor angle, contrarian threads. Use these to sharpen questions without reciting them.

**Examples:**
- Dominant narrative → "Everyone's saying X. Is that actually true?"
- The gap → "Nobody's talking about Y. Why do you think that is?"
- Key stats → "The numbers say Z. Does that match what you see on the ground?"
- Competitor angle → "They framed it as A. What's the piece they're missing?"
- Contrarian threads → "I found some debate around B. Where do you land on that?"

Research makes questions sharper. It doesn't replace them.

---

## Pattern Selection Guide

| Topic Source | Primary Pattern | Secondary Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Trend I found | "Why Now" | "So What" |
| Competitor article | "Counter-Angle" | "They're Wrong About" |
| User's idea | "Pull the Thread" | "Only I Can Say This" |
| Case study / win | "How It Actually Happened" | "What This Proves" |
| Contrarian take | "The Emperor Has No Clothes" | "Here's What They Miss" |

---

## Pattern: "Why Now" (Trend Topics)

**Use when:** The agent surfaces an industry trend, news item, or pattern.

**Opening:** "I noticed [trend]. Here's why it matters right now: [one-line framing]. Want to dig into this?"

**Interview questions:**
- "What's your first reaction to this — is it real or noise?"
- "Who does this hurt? Who does this help?"
- "Have you seen early signs of this with any clients?"
- "What's the move people SHOULD make but won't?"
- "If this trend plays out fully, what does the world look like in 12 months?"

**Angle emerges from:** User's contrarian take on whether the trend is real + concrete evidence from their work.

---

## Pattern: "Counter-Angle" (Competitor Response)

**Use when:** A competitor publishes something worth responding to.

**Opening:** "[Competitor] just published [topic]. Their angle is [summary]. Our take would be [differentiated angle] — because [one reason]."

**Interview questions:**
- "What do they get right? (Be fair — credibility requires acknowledging their wins.)"
- "What's the big thing they're missing or wrong about?"
- "Why can you say this and they can't? What's your unique vantage point?"
- "What would happen if someone followed their advice vs. yours?"
- "Is this worth naming them directly, or is it better to address the idea without naming?"

**Angle emerges from:** The gap between their take and User's take, backed by User's direct experience.

---

## Pattern: "Pull the Thread" (User's Idea)

**Use when:** User drops a seed — a thought, frustration, observation.

**Opening:** "That's interesting. The blog could be: [one-line thesis]. Let me pull this thread a bit."

**Interview questions:**
- "Where did this observation come from — something you saw, something that happened, or something that's been building?"
- "What's the version of this that most people believe? And what's the real version?"
- "Can you give me a specific moment where this was true?"
- "What's at stake if someone gets this wrong?"
- "Is this something you've believed for a while, or is it a new realization?"

**Angle emerges from:** The specific story or moment that sparked the idea + why User sees it differently.

---

## Pattern: "How It Actually Happened" (Case Study / Win)

**Use when:** There's a concrete win, client result, or milestone to write about.

**Opening:** "This is a case study in disguise. The surface story is [what happened]. The real story is probably [deeper pattern]."

**Interview questions:**
- "Walk me through the moment it clicked. What was the before and after?"
- "What did you try that failed first? (Failures make case studies credible.)"
- "What's the number? Revenue, time saved, conversion change — give me something concrete."
- "What's the principle here that applies beyond this specific case?"
- "Who needs to hear this most? Who's in the exact 'before' state right now?"

**Angle emerges from:** Specific numbers + the principle that generalizes + before/after contrast.

---

## Pattern: "The Emperor Has No Clothes" (Contrarian Take)

**Use when:** User has a strong opinion that goes against industry consensus.

**Opening:** "So you're saying [consensus belief] is wrong. The real story is [User's take]. That's a contrarian blog."

**Interview questions:**
- "Why does everyone believe the wrong thing? What's the source of the myth?"
- "What's the cost of believing it? Who's paying that cost right now?"
- "When did you first realize this was wrong?"
- "What's the better way? Be specific — not just 'do it differently' but 'do THIS instead.'"
- "Who's going to hate this blog? (If nobody hates it, it's not contrarian enough.)"

**Angle emerges from:** The myth's origin story + the cost of believing it + the specific alternative.

---

## Universal Follow-Up Questions

These work across all patterns when you need to go deeper:

- "Say more about that."
- "What's an example?"
- "Why does that matter?"
- "What's the counter-argument?"
- "So the blog is basically: [thesis]. Missing anything?"

---

## When to Stop Interviewing

Stop when you have ALL three:

1. **A clear thesis** — one sentence that captures the argument
2. **Concrete evidence** — at least one specific story, number, or example
3. **A strong opinion** — User's genuine take, not balanced "on one hand..."

If you have all three after 2 questions, stop. Don't over-interview.
