# Headline Craft — Ghost-Writer

Titles are 80% of whether the piece gets read. A world-class blog with a weak title is a tree falling in an empty forest.

## The 3-Second Test

If your ideal reader sees this title in their feed, do they stop? If not, the title fails.

## Title Rules

- Primary keyword MUST appear
- 50-65 characters (Google truncates longer)
- No periods at end
- Odd numbers outperform even
- Negative framing outperforms positive ("You're Doomed" > "How to Succeed")

---

## Headline Formulas

### Formula 1: The Contrarian Title

**Pattern:** "[Keyword]: [What Everyone Believes] — [Why They're Wrong]"

**Examples:**
- "Revenue Systems: Most Agencies Sell Websites — Here's Why That's Wrong"
- "SEO: Everyone Chases Rankings — They Should Chase Revenue"
- "AI Builders: They'll Replace Designers — But Not Architects"

**Best for:** Contrarian takes, myth-busting

---

### Formula 2: The Kill Shot Title

**Pattern:** "[Keyword]: [The One Thing — Stated as Threat or Promise]"

**Examples:**
- "Revenue Systems: Your Website Is Costing You Customers"
- "SEO: You're Invisible Where It Matters Most"
- "AI Mentions: If You're Not Showing Up, Your Competitor Is"

**Best for:** Pieces with a single sharp thesis

---

### Formula 3: The News Anchor Title

**Pattern:** "[Number] [Thing Happened]. [What It Means]."

**Examples:**
- "4 AI Builders Tested. None Generated Revenue."
- "1,000 Designers Fired. Here's What They Know That You Don't."
- "$5M Revenue Target. One Website Made It Possible."

**Best for:** News-driven, trend commentary

---

### Formula 4: The Question Title

**Pattern:** "[Question That Creates Tension]? [Hint at Answer]"

**Examples:**
- "Why Does Your Website Bring Zero Leads? (It's Not What You Think)"
- "Are You a Brick-Maker or an Architect? Only One Survives"
- "What Happens When AI Recommends Your Competitor?"

**Best for:** Conceptual pieces, paradigm shifts

**Anti-pattern:** Questions answerable with "no" ("Is AI Going to Change Web Design?" — yes, obviously, scroll past)

---

### Formula 5: The How-To Title (With Edge)

**Pattern:** "How to [Achieve Outcome] — [Unexpected Method or Constraint]"

**Examples:**
- "How to Build a Revenue System — Start With the Pipeline, Not the Design"
- "How to Get AI Mentions — Stop Writing for Humans"
- "How to Rank #1 on Google — Build for AI First"

**Best for:** Practical pieces, "here's the move" posts

---

### Formula 6: The List Title (Odd Numbers Only)

**Pattern:** "[Number] [Things] [Unexpected Framing]"

**Examples:**
- "3 Reasons Your Website Doesn't Convert (And 1 Fix That Works)"
- "5 Ways AI Is Already Stealing Your Customers"
- "7 Agency Lies About SEO"

**Best for:** Digestible, scannable pieces

**Rule:** Odd numbers only. 3, 5, 7, 9. Never 4, 6, 8, 10.

---

### Formula 7: The "Why" Title

**Pattern:** "Why [Surprising Thing] — [The Real Reason]"

**Examples:**
- "Why Pretty Websites Don't Pay Bills — And What Does"
- "Why Your Competitor Ranks Higher — It's Not SEO"
- "Why ChatGPT Recommends Some Businesses — But Not Yours"

**Best for:** "The real story" angles, hidden causes

---

## Title Testing Method

For every blog, generate 5-7 titles using at least 3 different formulas.

### The Scroll Test
Would the ideal reader stop scrolling? Rank 1-7.

### The Promise Test
Does the blog deliver what the title promises? If the title promises "You're Doomed" and the blog is balanced analysis, the title over-promised. Kill it.

### The Curiosity Gap Test
Does the title create a gap between what the reader knows and what they want to know?

### The Specificity Test
Could this title apply to 100 other blog posts? If yes, it's too generic.

### The Keyword Test
Is the primary keyword front-loaded?

## Title Presentation Format

When presenting titles to the user, group by formula:

```
## Formula 2 — Kill Shot Titles
1. Revenue Systems: Your Website Is Costing You Customers
2. Revenue Systems: Stop Losing Leads to a Digital Brochure

## Formula 3 — News Anchor Titles
3. 4 AI Builders Tested. None Generated Revenue.

## Formula 1 — Contrarian Titles
4. Revenue Systems: Most Agencies Sell Websites — Here's Why That's Wrong
5. SEO: Everyone Chases Rankings — They Should Chase Revenue

## Formula 4 — Question Titles
6. Why Does Your Website Bring Zero Leads? (It's Not What You Think)
7. Are You a Brick-Maker or an Architect?
```

User picks one.

## Title Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

- "The Ultimate Guide to..." (overused, promises too much)
- "Everything You Need to Know About..." (same problem)
- "X: A Comprehensive Overview" (academic, not scroll-stopping)
- "Thoughts on X" (no promise, no curiosity gap)
- "My Take on X" (unless you're famous)
- Titles with colons that have weak second halves
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver ("You Won't Believe What AI Did!")
- Single-word titles ("Disruption") — not enough information
