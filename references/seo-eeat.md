# SEO & EEAT — Ghost-Writer

How to make world-class writing rank. EEAT = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness.

## On-Page SEO Checklist

### Title (H1)
- Primary keyword front-loaded
- 50-65 characters (Google truncates longer)
- No periods at end
- Odd numbers outperform even in list titles

### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Promise, not summary
- Formula: "[Keyword] is [what's happening]. Here's [what you'll learn] — and [why it matters]."

### URL Slug
- Keyword only, hyphens
- No dates, no numbers, no stop words
- Example: `/revenue-generating-websites` not `/the-revenue-generating-websites-2026-june`

### Heading Hierarchy
- H1: Title (one per page)
- H2: Major sections (4-6 per post)
- H3: Sub-sections only if needed
- Keywords in H2s where natural

### Keyword Placement
- **Primary keyword:** Title, first 100 words, one H2, meta, slug
- **Secondary keywords:** Naturally in body
- **LSI keywords:** Related terms Google expects (for "revenue generating website": SEO, leads, conversion, service business)

### Internal Linking
- 2-3 links to other relevant posts
- Link text descriptive, not "click here"

### Image Alt Text
- Descriptive, keyword where natural
- Not keyword stuffing

## EEAT: The Four Pillars

### Experience (First-Hand)
**Signals:**
- First-person concrete stories
- Specific details only a practitioner knows
- Mistakes made and learned from
- "I saw," "We built," "We learned"

**Anti-signals:**
- "Many experts believe..."
- "Studies have shown..." (without naming)
- Abstract claims without personal grounding

**Rule:** Every major claim must be grounded in direct experience OR a named, linked source.

### Expertise (Depth)
**Signals:**
- Technical precision
- Nuance ("It depends" is more expert than "Always do X")
- Counter-arguments addressed fairly
- Reader learns something they couldn't Google in 30 seconds

**Techniques:**
- **Depth Drop:** One paragraph going technically deeper
- **Jargon Check:** Use terms correctly, explain once, then use freely
- **Contrarian Nuance:** "Most people say X. True in A and B. Fails in C."

### Authoritativeness (Recognized Voice)
**Signals:**
- Unique vantage point
- Original insights, not repackaged consensus
- Confident assertions without hedging
- Track record visible

**Techniques:**
- **Prediction Callback:** Reference something you predicted that came true
- **Insider Reveal:** Something only an insider would know
- **Named Opposition:** "Wix won't tell you this. Here's what actually happens."

### Trustworthiness (Accuracy)
**Signals:**
- Named sources with links
- Specific numbers, not rounded
- Transparency about limitations
- Corrections when wrong
- Clear fact/opinion distinction

**Techniques:**
- **Source Link:** "CNBC reported" > "It was reported that"
- **Honesty Admission:** "I don't know if this works for X. We've only tested Y."
- **Specific Number:** "1,000 jobs" not "thousands of jobs"
- **Date Stamp:** "As of June 2026"

## EEAT Writing Checklist

Before finalizing:

**Experience:**
- [ ] At least one first-person story
- [ ] Specific detail only a practitioner would know
- [ ] No floating assertions

**Expertise:**
- [ ] One "depth drop" paragraph
- [ ] Technical terms used correctly
- [ ] Reader learns something new

**Authoritativeness:**
- [ ] One original insight
- [ ] Confident assertions, no hedging
- [ ] Unique vantage point clear

**Trustworthiness:**
- [ ] Stats have named sources
- [ ] Numbers specific, not rounded
- [ ] Clear fact/opinion distinction
- [ ] Date context where relevant

**On-Page SEO:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title, first 100 words, one H2
- [ ] Meta description written (150-160 chars)
- [ ] URL slug proposed
- [ ] 2-3 internal link opportunities
- [ ] Heading hierarchy clean
